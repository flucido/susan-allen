## T1: Clove (Clove Therapy) — "a mental health collective"

> **Live demo:** https://clove-fluid-demo.squarespace.com/
> **Tagline:** "Introducing Clove, a mental health collective."
> **Pages:** About, Services, Team, Blog, Contact

### Structural fit (high level)

This is the most purpose-built template in the shortlist. The landing page includes:
- "Our approach" (WFC Principle 4 framing — emotional calibration)
- "Providing you with a safe space to relax" (Principle 1 — sensory safety)
- "In-person and remote options" (Susan has both)
- 4 services (Therapy, Psychiatry, Coaching, Acupuncture) — Susan has 1 service with 11 specialty areas; needs "Specialties" page rather than service list
- Team page with 4 practitioners (PSY.D, MD, PHD, ND, LAc) — credentials prominently displayed. **This is the killer feature for Susan — ASRM/PSI deserve exactly this treatment.**
- Testimonial: "Clove helped me find a therapist..." — Susan can swap
- Blog: 4 posts in 3 categories — Susan has 3 posts
- "Get started with Clove, today." with "BOOK A CONSULTATION" CTA — swap to email per SOW

### 6-gate evaluation

| Gate | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| **1. Element Count** | 🟡 MIXED | Home page is well-balanced (looks like 12-15 elements). Team page is dense (4 cards × 4 elements + section dividers = 16+ per visible state — borderline OK). |
| **2. Arousal Calibration** | ✅ LIKELY PASS | Visual review needed, but the "safe space" language and the team-page calm-tone suggests low-arousal palette. Need to confirm on actual screenshot. |
| **3. Revisability** | ✅ PASS | No forms on home, but contact has standard form pattern. No irreversible actions. |
| **4. Dominance Preservation** | ✅ PASS | "Get started with Clove, today" is invitational, not demanding. No urgency. No dark patterns. |
| **5. Implicit Aesthetic** | ✅ LIKELY PASS | Hierarchy appears clean (H2 for sections, H4 for posts). Grid is intentional. |
| **6. Accessibility** | ❓ NEEDS DEEPER TEST | Default Squarespace 7.1 — generally good, but the H2 used for everything (including team member names) might be an over-use. |

### WFC framework strengths
- **Specialty-prioritized team page** (ASRM/PSI = exactly this treatment)
- **WFC archetype alignment:** Landing (calm orientation), Content (About/Approach), Conversion (Services + Get Started), Content (Blog)
- **"Pillars of care" / "Our approach" framing** translates to WFC's 6 principles
- **Testimonial pattern** — Susan can replace with a real client quote
- **In-person + remote options** copy is already there

### What needs adaptation for Susan
- **Solo-practitioner voice:** All current copy assumes a group practice. Susan is solo. ~70% of copy needs to swap "we" → "I", and "team" → "Susan's credentials".
- **Services page → Specialties page:** Clove's 4 service cards don't map to Susan's 11 specialty areas. Better to do a specialties page with 11 cards or a "hub + spoke" structure.
- **CTA:** "BOOK A CONSULTATION" → "Email Susan" per SOW out-of-scope (no online booking)
- **Map at footer:** Not relevant for Susan (no physical office per dossier — in-person at SF/Marin locations unspecified, telehealth CA-wide)
- **Color palette:** Clove's default may not match WFC's Calm Base. Needs visual review of the actual rendered template.

### Estimated adaptation cost
**Low-medium.** Structure is right; copy and a few CTA swaps are the bulk. Worth the 0.5–1.0 hr of Milestone 3 (template config) in the Phase 1 plan.

## T2: Jenani — "Holistic healer"

> **Live demo:** https://www.squarespace.com/templates/jenani-fluid-demo
> **Tagline:** "Holistic healer"
> **Pages:** Home, About, Services (Pricing, Schedule)

### Structural fit (high level)

Strong fit for solo practitioner with services + values. Landing page includes:
- H1: "Holistic healer"
- H4: "About me" (subtitle pattern, not section break — unusual but works)
- H2: Service description
- H4: "My services" with "Schedule a session" CTA
- H1: "Healing is for everyone ·"
- H2: Service areas (Psychological, Physical, Immunity, Urinary & reproductive, Recovery)
- H4: "Pillars of care" (Intentional / Affirming / Generative) — strong values frame
- H4: "Frequently asked questions" with 4 expandable items
- H4: "Still have questions? Reach out!" with contact form

### 6-gate evaluation

| Gate | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| **1. Element Count** | ✅ PASS | H1 + H4 (about/services/FAQ) + H2 (services list) = clean, sparse. Likely 12-15 elements. |
| **2. Arousal Calibration** | 🟡 MIXED | H1 "Holistic healer" reads as warm/grounded. The big H1 "Healing is for everyone" is large text but mixed case — calmer than Clove's. |
| **3. Revisability** | ✅ PASS | FAQ is expand/collapse (low cognitive load). Form is multi-step. |
| **4. Dominance Preservation** | ✅ PASS | "Still have questions? Reach out!" is invitational. No urgency. |
| **5. Implicit Aesthetic** | ✅ LIKELY PASS | H4 used as section labels keeps H1/H2 reserved for content. Clean. |
| **6. Accessibility** | ❓ NEEDS DEEPER TEST | Standard Squarespace 7.1. |

### WFC framework strengths
- **FAQ block as expand/collapse** — matches the WFC SOW's "FAQ block structured for AI citation" requirement (Milestone 4)
- **"Pillars of care" section** maps directly to WFC's 6 principles — easy translation
- **Solo-practitioner voice** built in ("About me", "My services")
- **Service areas** (Psychological, Physical, etc.) — could map to Susan's 11 specialty areas
- **Form is multi-step** — revisitability built in
- **"Healing is for everyone"** — invitational, not institutional

### What needs adaptation for Susan
- **"Holistic healer" copy → "Reproductive + perinatal mental health"** — title swap, not structural
- **Schedule/booking integration** is built in (Schedule link in nav) — needs removal per SOW out-of-scope
- **Pricing page** in nav — Susan doesn't publish pricing, so remove
- **5 service areas → 11 specialty areas** — could expand or restructure
- **Color palette** — needs visual review

### Estimated adaptation cost
**Low.** Most copy is in solo-practitioner voice already. Structural changes (removing pricing, removing booking) are minor.

## T3: Clune — "boutique spa"

> **Live demo:** https://www.squarespace.com/templates/clune-fluid-demo
> **Tagline:** "A boutique spa specializing in advanced skincare techniques."
> **Pages:** Services, About, Team, Contact

### Structural fit (high level)

Clean structure, **but the "boutique spa" copy flavor doesn't translate to therapist voice.** Pages:
- Services with bold arrows ("Facials ⟶", "Body Treatments ⟶")
- "Pamper your skin with products that are safe enough to eat" — H1 statement. Spa marketing voice. **Doesn't fit Susan.**
- Newsletter signup
- Color palette options include "Light Gray, Light Pink, Sage Green" — **best palette match for WFC Calm Base of any shortlist template**
- Has Book Appointment CTA

### 6-gate evaluation

| Gate | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| **1. Element Count** | ✅ PASS | Sparse layout, likely 10-12 elements. |
| **2. Arousal Calibration** | ❌ FAIL | "Pamper your skin with products that are safe enough to eat" — high-arousal marketing language, demands attention. Conflicts with WFC Principle 4. |
| **3. Revisability** | ✅ PASS | Standard. |
| **4. Dominance Preservation** | 🟡 MIXED | "Book Appointment" is mildly demanding; better would be "Schedule a Consultation" or "Email to schedule". |
| **5. Implicit Aesthetic** | 🟡 MIXED | Clean grid, but the H1 statement is overly punchy. |
| **6. Accessibility** | ❓ NEEDS DEEPER TEST | Standard. |

### WFC framework strengths
- **Color palette option** "Light Gray, Light Pink, Sage Green" is the closest match to WFC's Calm Base (peachy cream + sage green). Best palette match.
- **Sparse layout** — respects 9-17 element constraint
- **Simple structure** — Services, About, Team, Contact, plus optional newsletter

### What needs adaptation for Susan
- **Replace ALL the spa-flavored copy** — the entire "Pamper your skin..." angle has to go
- **Service categories** (Facials, Body Treatments) → Susan's specialty areas
- **"Boutique spa" branding** → "Reproductive + perinatal mental health practice"
- **Team page** — for solo Susan, this becomes a credential showcase (not a team list)
- **Book Appointment CTA** → "Email Susan" per SOW out-of-scope

### Estimated adaptation cost
**Medium-high.** The structure is right, but the copy flavor is wrong. Probably 1.5-2.0 hrs of copy replacement.

## T4: Aurora — "Wellness Retreat"

> **Live demo:** https://www.squarespace.com/templates/aurora-fluid-demo
> **Tagline:** "Wellness Retreat"
> **Pages:** About, Services, Booking, Cart (e-commerce)

### Structural fit (high level)

Visually warm, gradient-friendly, "wellness retreat" aesthetic. Big section dividers (H1: "Energy", "Healing") give it a strong identity. **But it's built for e-commerce / retreat-booking, which Susan explicitly does NOT want per SOW.**

Pages: About, Services, Cart (with date pickers for booking, "BOOK EXPERIENCE" CTA, "0 items in cart" indicator in nav). This is a retreat-booking template.

### 6-gate evaluation

| Gate | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| **1. Element Count** | 🟡 MIXED | Home is balanced, but the booking sections (date pickers, quantity, "Book Retreat" buttons) inflate element count quickly. |
| **2. Arousal Calibration** | 🟡 MIXED | "Energy" / "Healing" H1 section dividers are high-arousal typography. They work for "wellness retreat" but feel too marketing-y for a clinical practice. |
| **3. Revisability** | ✅ PASS | Standard. |
| **4. Dominance Preservation** | 🟡 MIXED | "BOOK EXPERIENCE" is mildly demanding. Booking flow has urgency-adjacent copy. |
| **5. Implicit Aesthetic** | ✅ PASS | Clean visual structure, intentional section dividers. |
| **6. Accessibility** | ❓ NEEDS DEEPER TEST | Standard. |

### WFC framework strengths
- **Visual warmth** — gradients, photography
- **"Healing" / "Energy" framing** — could translate to Susan's clinical voice with rewrites

### What needs adaptation for Susan
- **Remove e-commerce infrastructure** — cart, date pickers, "BOOK EXPERIENCE" CTA, "0 items in cart" nav
- **Replace retreat copy** — entire "Wellness Retreat" angle has to go
- **Service architecture** (accommodations, instructors, digital detox) → Susan's specialty areas
- **Cost** — removing Squarespace Commerce is moderate effort; turning off the booking flow is more involved

### Estimated adaptation cost
**High.** E-commerce removal alone is 1.0-1.5 hrs. Plus the copy rewrite. Not recommended unless visual warmth is the decisive factor.
