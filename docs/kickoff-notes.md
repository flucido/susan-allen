## 1. Summaries

### What Happened (the short version)

### Sensory Layer (200ms test + preconscious aesthetics)

- **200ms test** — current site fails: header has placeholder text (street address, phone — may already be fixed, not sure), old template residue, focal point not strong, palette is "calming but too low arousal" (Susan's preference for minimalism doesn't require "lack of any underlying design").
- **Focal point** — Hero text centered + large, but immediately below it sit two competing service links side by side + a third CTA further down. Information architecture could be cleaner.
- **Color palette + fonts** — system is "super coherent" overall; Source Sans Pro is fine, typography is OK; no need to change. Small inconsistency: Arial fallback in some browsers (template default; can be fixed in template).
- **Color discipline + WCAG / ADA** — contrast levels for links/backgrounds/hovers **don't pass WCAG**, "and some of those are now moving from recommendations into ADA compliance issues." Affects SEO + agent/chatbot findability: "you can't be picked up by some chat bots" if you don't pass standard WCAG. Susan acknowledged ("there were, like, a light blue or something, I think") — high-priority fix.
- **Information density** — uniform canvas, uniform padding, no card/box inconsistencies. Fine.
- **Micro-interactions** — no hover effects or motion; small reward mechanic that increases engagement. Worth adding.

### Emotional Layer

- **Color scheme** — currently "monochrome" and "falls a little bit flat" — doesn't cause the engagement arousal we want. Can stay monochrome but improve how colors work together.
- **Font weights** — minor work to give it more emotional impact.

### Meaning Layer (engagement + brand voice + clinical content)

- **Contact page** — currently says "my practice is currently full." Lauren recommends: (a) soften the wording, or (b) remove it + keep at least an email contact path. Susan agreed the abruptness is off. **My recommendation would be to have at least an email, allow someone to contact you via email.**
- **Professional headshot** — needs refresh. Susan has iPhone + her daughter; can DIY at home.
- **Psychology Today badge** — Susan has one; bring it into the aesthetic so it's not just "a badge sitting there."
- **Footer** — licensing info is left-justified while the rest of the page is center-justified. Small inconsistency to fix.
- **Biophilic photography integration** — current photos (sunset with tree, office interior) are "lovely" but "in a box right now." Blend them into the aesthetic, don't just float them above the text.
- **Brand consistency for reproductive/perinatal specialty** — the current sunset + tulip photography doesn't clearly connect to infertility / reproductive health counseling. Susan agreed: "I'm not sure why I'm looking at Tulips for infertility and reproductive health counseling." Recommendation: pick photography that feels comforting for people navigating fertility / pregnancy / postpartum — and integrate it with the text rather than floating above.
- **Footer left-vs-center inconsistency** — small but matters for engagement.

- **Static-site option** — Susan's site is simple enough to serve for free as a static site. Would cost-save the $228/yr Squarespace renewal. **Not for this engagement** — Lauren floated as "something to think about down the road," not pushed.
- **Generative search shift** — top-of-page click-through rate has dropped from ~80% (a year+ ago) to ~28%. Below position 4, traditional SEO is "almost minuscule." Generative search drives a different engagement pattern.
- **Susan's recent findability** — used to rank top for "San Francisco infertility therapists," now nowhere on Google. Was recently surfaced as one of three in a ChatGPT query for "infertility therapist San Francisco" — Lauren: "That's what you want!" Susan: "I don't know why I was one of them. I find it very odd."
