### 0:35–0:45 (10 min) — OKF introduction
**Lead: Frank**

- **What it is:** Open Knowledge Format (OKF) — one file = one concept. Plain markdown + YAML frontmatter. Vendor-neutral, AI-readable.
- **Why it matters for Susan:** Her expertise (ASRM/PSI credentials, reproductive + perinatal mental health) is the differentiator. OKF gives her a structure where (a) she can maintain it herself after we hand off, (b) it stays portable if she ever swaps platforms, (c) AI engines can read it cleanly.

- **The hook:** This is the part most therapist websites skip. The reason Susan's site will rank better than her peers isn't more blog posts — it's that her content is structured for the way AI engines actually read. That's the WFC differentiator.

## Why This Agenda Looks The Way It Does

> Reasoning for Frank's review — cut or expand as you see fit.

1. **Frank leads everything, but Lauren is named once at the top.** Susan signed up for a two-person team, not just Frank. The 5-second team-intro at 0:00–0:05 keeps that honest without forcing Lauren's presence into a meeting she's not in.
2. **Hour counts are NOT the headline.** The 8-hour floor is a real constraint and Susan knows it. Putting it in plain English at minute 0:10 is more honest than burying it. But the milestone walk is the spine of the meeting, not the budget.
3. **OKF gets its own block (10 min).** This is the WFC differentiator. If Susan leaves remembering one thing, it should be "I own the work, and the structure makes me findable." Most therapist websites don't have this conversation. We do.
4. **Phase 2 is named, not pitched.** Susan needs to know the SEO/AI findability work continues. Naming it without selling it keeps trust intact — the on-site 6/30 conversation set this up, the SOW rev. 3 numbers ($1,600–$3,000) already landed in the 7/7 reply. The kick-off closes the loop: it's there, we'll talk about it after Phase 1, no pressure today.
5. **Inputs section comes AFTER scope + milestones.** If we ask "what photos do you have?" before Susan knows what we're building, the inputs feel like homework. After she sees the path, the inputs feel like participation.
6. **The 7-day pause policy is named explicitly.** WFC's onboarding guide says it; the kick-off says it. Susan is busy, solo-practice, reproductive/perinatal caseload is intense. We are not going to ghost her; we are also not going to chase her. The pause policy is a boundary that protects both sides.
7. **60 minutes is the right default.** WFC's guide says "short and focused" meetings. 60 min lets us cover all five blocks with room for Q&A. If Susan wants to go longer, we extend. If she wants to wrap in 45, we cut the inputs section early.
8. **Discovery session is a separate action item, not part of this meeting.** Lauren confirmed Tue 10:00. Frank + Lauren align on 2-3 specific slot options this week. Susan picks. Discovery is 30 min, content-focused, owned by Lauren.
9. **Post-meeting deliverables are pre-written.** Frank shouldn't be figuring out "what's next" the morning after. Squarespace access request, Discovery slot shortlist, kick-off summary — all 24-hour-after actions, owned, dated.
