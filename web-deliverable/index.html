Part 1

What we found in your current site

We walked susanallentherapy.com through the WFC 6-gate framework — the same checklist we use on every build. Your site is functional and the photography is professional, but two things are actively working against you.

Element count
High fail
About page has 25+ elements; Focus page 30+. Above the 9–17 range where anxious users stay oriented.
Arousal calibration
Medium
ALL CAPS H1 reads institutional, not warm. Inconsistent hierarchy.
Implicit aesthetic
High fail
H1 is your brand name, not the page content. Users scan for "what do you treat," not "who are you."
Accessibility
Needs a pass
Color contrast fails WCAG AA. Affects SEO + AI findability, not just screen readers.
Dominance
Pass
No urgency cues, no pressure. Good — we'll preserve this.
Revisability
N/A
No forms (email intake). We'll keep it that way per your HIPAA workflow.

The three things we'll fix first

  • High Services page silently redirects to home. Worse than a 404 — users don't know they didn't get the page. We'll rebuild this as your Specialties page.
  • High H1 is your brand on every page. "SUSAN ALLEN LMFT | PSYCHOTHERAPY" doesn't tell a visitor what you treat. We'll make every H1 about the page content.
  • Medium Color contrast fails WCAG AA. Affects whether AI chatbots can surface your site in results — not just a screen-reader concern.
  • Quick win ALL CAPS H1 + footer alignment. Small visual fixes that shift the feel from institutional to warm.

Part 3

Three template mockups — built with your content

These aren't the Squarespace template demos. We took three templates that fit "warm, specialty health practice" and rebuilt each one with your actual content, your specialties, your credentials, and your contact info — so you can see how your site would feel in each direction. Click any one to open it.

Primary recommendation

Clove — the mental-health template

Built for mental health practices. The team-page pattern surfaces your ASRM + PSI credentials prominently — your single biggest differentiator. Calm, structured, clinical-but-warm. Needs a small copy pass to sound like a solo practitioner rather than a collective.

Best for: credential prominence · specialty structure · testimonial pattern

View Clove mockup →
Backup · the personal option

Jenani — the solo-practitioner template

First-person voice built in ("About me", "My services"). Has an expand/collapse FAQ block — a SOW requirement we'd otherwise have to build from scratch. The quietest, most personal of the three.

Best for: solo voice · built-in FAQ · first-person warmth

View Jenani mockup →
Third option · the minimal one

Clune — the minimal editorial template

The most restrained of the three — generous whitespace, sage-forward palette. Closest to "I just like really beautiful, minimal things." We translate the spa-flavored default copy into clinical warmth; the structure stays.

Best for: minimal aesthetic · generous whitespace · sage palette

View Clune mockup →

How to pick: Open all three, scroll through each one. You don't have to pick the "best" one — pick the one that feels closest to how you want a potential client to feel when they land on your site. You can also tell me "I like the header from Clove and the FAQ from Jenani" — we mix and match.
