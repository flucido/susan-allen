Monotone Palette Review

Six cool-hued monotone color systems for the Susan Allen therapy site — each a single cool hue family with one deliberate pop of color as the accent.

ClientSusan Allen, LMFT
Prepared byWellfull Collective
DateJuly 2026
Palettes6 monotone variants

How this works

Six cool-monotone color systems, each mapped to the same six-token structure so they compare apples-to-apples. Every palette holds a single cool hue family across bg, surface, fg, muted, and border — then breaks the monotone with one saturated pop as the accent. This is round one — directional, not binding. Swipe through to see how each palette feels applied to real site elements, then tell us what resonates and what to explore further in the next round.

Step 01 Review each palette Six slides, each with token swatches and a mock application — nav, hero, card, and pull quote rendered in that palette's actual colors. Watch how the accent pop reads against the cool-monotone base.
Step 02 Compare side-by-side A single grid puts all six accent bars and mini-swatches next to each other for quick visual scanning of the cool families and pop colors.
Step 03 Share your reactions Tell us what feels right, what feels off, and what you'd like to see more of. Blend suggestions welcome — e.g. "B's monotone base with D's pop color."
Step 04 Iterate, then converge We'll refine based on your feedback and tighten the options over the next round. Nothing here is locked — typography (Cormorant Garamond + Atkinson Hyperlegible) is held constant across variants only so the comparison is fair, not because it's final.
